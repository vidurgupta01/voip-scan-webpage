VoIP/RTC Infrastructure Measurement Study

Academic research on Internet-facing real-time communication infrastructure

What is this?

If you're seeing traffic from our scanning infrastructure, this page explains what we're doing and why.

We are conducting an academic research study measuring the deployment and security posture of Voice over IP (VoIP) and real-time communication (RTC) infrastructure on the public Internet.

What we're doing

  • Sending SIP OPTIONS requests to discover VoIP endpoints
  • Collecting response headers to understand deployment patterns
  • Analyzing encryption adoption (SIP-TLS, SRTP)
  • Mapping the global RTC infrastructure landscape

This is NOT an attack

Our scanning is passive and non-intrusive:
  • No authentication attempts or credential testing
  • No calls placed or media sessions established
  • No exploitation of vulnerabilities
  • No collection of personally identifiable information
  • Only metadata (IP, port, banner) is recorded

Research Purpose

This study aims to:

  • Create the first comprehensive census of Internet-facing RTC infrastructure
  • Understand security posture of critical voice infrastructure
  • Identify encryption adoption rates
  • Inform the security community about potential risks

Findings will be published in an academic paper and any critical vulnerabilities will be responsibly disclosed.
